Technical signals computed live in your browser: Twelve Data or Finnhub if you add a key, otherwise a public Yahoo Finance relay. Plus a shared US Dollar Index (DXY) read and the ForexFactory economic calendar. No account required, no broker connection, no server — everything runs on this page. Not financial advice; verify any setup yourself and always trade with a stop loss.

All times shown are in UAE time (Asia/Dubai), regardless of your device's own timezone setting.

Each chart plots Entry/SL/TP1/TP2/TP3 directly on the 15m candles (dashed while unconfirmed, solid once a signal fires). 1H sets the bias and must be sitting in a zone, and the 15m trigger accepts either a candle pattern or a Smart Money structure break — Daily and 4H are still fetched and shown as context (Trend tab, confidence score) but no longer required for a signal to fire. TP1/TP2/TP3 target a configurable pip range (Size tab — default 100/200 pips at $0.10 per pip, i.e. $10-$20) rather than a pure risk-multiple, with a rough reachability read based on recent 15m volatility. Each read also checks for Smart Money Concepts (Fair Value Gaps, Order Blocks, Break of Structure / Change of Character) and rolls everything into a weighted 0-100 confidence score with a visible breakdown of what's contributing to it, including an automatic penalty when a major news release is imminent. The Trend / Size / Journal / News / Backtest tools are grouped into one panel below — switch tabs instead of scrolling through five separate cards.

Only one open trade per asset at a time. Once a signal fires for Gold or Silver, no new signal (or notification) fires for that asset again until the open one hits its stop loss or TP1 — the card shows "in trade" with the original entry/SL/TP until then.

No trade is risk-free. The stop loss, position sizing, and journal here exist to help you control and measure risk, not eliminate it.

Alerts only fire while this page is open and your screen is on. A closed app or locked phone can't receive push notifications without a backend server watching the market on your behalf.

Auto-refreshes every 2 minutes. Running both metals with a Twelve Data key at this pace uses noticeably more of the free daily credit budget than before — if you hit the limit, it automatically falls back to Finnhub, then Yahoo, for the rest of the day.

If every card shows "data error" at once: you're viewing this inside a sandboxed preview (e.g. Claude's in-chat artifact panel) that blocks outbound network requests. Open the file directly in Safari/Chrome instead.
